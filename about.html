About

Screen clarity.
Without glasses.

DOA is a Windows desktop application that adjusts what your screen displays so that people with myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism, or presbyopia can see it clearly — without wearing glasses.

What DOA does

Most people with refractive errors can still see their screen — just not as well as they could. Text gets a little soft, fine detail blurs, and long sessions cause fatigue. DOA addresses this by applying a real-time correction layer to your entire screen, calibrated to your prescription.

You enter your eyeglass prescription once. DOA runs in the background and adjusts everything on your display — across every app, browser, document, and video — continuously, at up to 60 frames per second.

There is no hardware involved. Nothing is attached to your screen. The correction is entirely computational, applied before each frame is displayed.

Who it helps

DOA is most useful for everyday screen activity — reading, writing, browsing, and working. Anyone who regularly uses a computer without glasses can benefit.

📖

Reading without glasses

Articles, documents, and emails — comfortable to read without reaching for your glasses first.

💼

Long work sessions

Reduced eye strain during extended screen time, particularly for near-sighted users working up close.

👴

Presbyopia — reading in bed

Age-related near-vision loss makes reading screens uncomfortable at normal distances. DOA compensates for this directly.

🕶️

When glasses aren't available

Glasses left in another room, broken, or simply not wanted — DOA keeps your screen usable.

🌈

Color blindness

Daltonization modes for Protanopia, Deuteranopia, and Tritanopia — applied alongside any active prescription.

🖥️

Multiple monitors

Different correction settings per display — useful for mixed setups where screens are at different distances.

What we're working to improve

DOA is in early access. The correction works — but there is real room to make it more accurate, more personal, and available on more platforms. Here is what we are actively focused on.

More accurate correction at higher prescriptions

Current correction quality is strongest for mild to moderate prescriptions. Extending reliable accuracy to higher prescriptions — particularly beyond −6D — is an active priority.

Personalised calibration

Prescription values are a starting point, not the full picture. Factors like screen distance, lighting, and individual optical variation affect how well the correction works. We are building a calibration system that adapts to you specifically, not just your prescription on paper.

Better colour blindness correction

Current daltonization modes apply established colour matrices. We are researching approaches that go further — improving real-world colour discrimination beyond what standard matrices achieve.

macOS support

The Windows version is the foundation. macOS is next.

Correction without knowing your prescription

Not everyone has an up-to-date prescription or access to an optometrist. We want DOA to be usable through guided calibration alone, without requiring a formal prescription as input.

Enhancing digital vision for a clearer world.
